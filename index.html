Welcome to the AIR Lab

Department of Biological and Agricultural Engineering

College of Agriculture and Life Sciences, North Carolina State University

At the Artificial Intelligence and Robotics (AIR) Lab, we use AI, robotics, and innovative technologies to drive innovation in precision livestock farming and sustainable agriculture. We combine machine learning, automation, sensing technologies, and data-driven solutions to improve animal health, welfare, food quality, and environmental outcomes.

Our Research Interests:

  • Artificial intelligence (AI) and predictive analytics
  • Robotics, UGV, and UAV automation
  • Computer vision, sensors, and IoT technology
  • Animal behavior, welfare, food safety, and sustainability
  • Air quality, greenhouse gas emissions, and disease prevention

What We Work On:

  • Artificial intelligence and robotics
  • Computer vision and sensor technologies
  • Health monitoring for poultry, cattle, and swine
  • Real-time animal behavior and welfare monitoring
  • Automation in poultry and livestock research and processing
  • ML for precision feeding and breeding
  • Rapid, non-destructive food quality testing

We are excited to lead innovation at the intersection of livestock, technology, and sustainability. Thank you for visiting and welcome to the AIR Lab! If you are interested in joining our lab, please submit the google form: Google Form .

Principal Investigator (PI)

Ramesh Bahadur Bist, Ph.D.

Assistant Professor of Artificial Intelligence for Integrated Food and Animal Systems

Department of Biological and Agricultural Engineering, College of Agriculture and Life Sciences

Email: rbbist@ncsu.edu

Office: 3110 Faucette Drive, RM 190, Raleigh, NC 27695-7625

Phone: (919)-515-6724

Dr. Ramesh Bahadur Bist received his Ph.D. in Poultry Science from the University of Georgia in 2024 under the supervision of Dr. Lilong Chai. During his Ph.D., he worked extensively with AI tools and computer vision to detect, classify, and segment animal health, behavior, and welfare conditions in poultry. He also used a robotic dog to detect mortality in hens and locate floor eggs.

After his Ph.D., Dr. Bist joined the University of Arkansas in 2024 as a postdoctoral fellow, working on computer vision and robotics with Dr. Dongyi Wang. His work included developing auto-labeling and text-prompt-based behavior detection models, and applying hyperspectral and thermal sensors to detect meat defects and foreign materials. He contributed to an unmanned ground vehicle for autonomous navigation in poultry plants using SLAM.

At NC State, Dr. Bist leads a research program focused on machine learning, robotics, automation, and sensing for livestock farming. His goals include predicting livestock diseases, monitoring animal health, building affordable vehicles, creating mobile apps, and designing non-destructive food quality tests.

He has published in journals like Computers and Electronics in Agriculture, Poultry Science, and Journal of Environmental Management. Dr. Bist serves as a guest editor for special issues in Applied Sciences and AgriEngineering (MDPI) and reviews for journals including Nature Scientific Reports and Sensors.

Available Positions

We seek highly motivated Master's, Ph.D. students, and postdoctoral researchers to join our team starting Fall 2025 to Fall 2028 in the Department of Biological and Agricultural Engineering. The students will be expected to conduct research within the field of artificial intelligence, robotics, machine vision, infectious disease modeling, and automation for precision livestock farming. Students with strong backgrounds in AI, robotics, computational biology, engineering, or related fields are encouraged to apply. Experience in programming, machine learning, computer vision, or mathematical modeling is preferred. Ph.D. positions will be fully funded, including stipend, tuition, health insurance, and travel support. Postdoctoral positions offer a competitive salary plus benefits.

We encourage highly motivated individuals to apply and join our innovative research team. If you are interested in the position, please email rameshba.bist@gmail.com with their CV and research statement.
