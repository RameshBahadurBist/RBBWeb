Precision Livestock Farming with AI & Robotics

Welcome to the AIR Lab

Our Research Interests:

  • Artificial intelligence (AI) and predictive analytics
  • Robotics, unmanned ground (UGV), and aerial (UAV) vehicle automation
  • Computer vision, sensors, and IoT technologies
  • Big data analysis and hyperspectral imaging
  • Animal behavior, welfare, food safety, and sustainability
  • Air quality, greenhouse gas emissions, and disease prevention

What We Work On:

  • Predicting and controlling livestock diseases like Avian Influenza
  • Health monitoring for poultry, cattle, and swine
  • Building affordable unmanned ground and aerial vehicles
  • Mobile apps for real-time animal health and behavior monitoring
  • Machine learning models for precision feeding, breeding, and embryo sexing
  • New methods for rapid, non-destructive food quality testing

Department of Biological and Agricultural Engineering
College of Agriculture and Life Sciences, North Carolina State University

At the AIR Lab, we harness AI, robotics, and cutting-edge technologies to revolutionize precision livestock farming and sustainable agriculture. Our mission is to enhance animal health, welfare, food quality, and environmental sustainability through machine learning, automation, and data-driven solutions.

Join us in shaping the future of livestock farming with technology!

Principal Investigator (PI)

Ramesh Bahadur Bist, Ph.D.

Assistant Professor of Artificial Intelligence for Integrated Food and Animal Systems

Department of Biological and Agricultural Engineering, College of Agriculture and Life Sciences

Email: rameshba.bist@gmail.com

Office: 000-00-0000

Dr. Ramesh Bahadur Bist earned his Ph.D. in Poultry Science from the University of Georgia in 2024 under Dr. Lilong Chai. His doctoral research focused on AI and computer vision for detecting, classifying, and segmenting poultry health, behavior, and welfare conditions. He also pioneered the use of a robotic dog for detecting hen mortality and floor eggs.

Post-Ph.D., Dr. Bist joined the University of Arkansas in 2024 as a postdoctoral fellow under Dr. Dongyi Wang, advancing computer vision and robotics. His contributions included auto-labeling models, text-prompt-based behavior detection, and sensor technologies like hyperspectral imaging for detecting meat defects. He developed an autonomous unmanned ground vehicle for poultry processing plants using SLAM.

At NC State, Dr. Bist leads a robust research program in machine learning, robotics, automation, and sensing technologies for livestock farming. His goals include predicting livestock diseases, monitoring animal health, developing affordable unmanned vehicles, creating real-time health tracking apps, and designing non-destructive food quality tests.

Dr. Bist has published extensively in journals like Computers and Electronics in Agriculture, Artificial Intelligence in Agriculture, and Poultry Science. He serves as a guest editor for special issues in Applied Sciences and AgriEngineering (MDPI) and reviews for journals including Nature Scientific Reports and Sensors.

Available Positions

We seek highly motivated Master's, Ph.D. students, and postdoctoral researchers to join our team starting Fall 2025 to Fall 2028 in the Department of Biological and Agricultural Engineering. Candidates with strong backgrounds in AI, robotics, computational biology, engineering, or related fields are encouraged to apply.
